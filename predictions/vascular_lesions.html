Vascular Lesion

Description

Red or purple spots caused by abnormal blood vessel growth, typically harmless. If you notice changes or have concerns, consult a healthcare professional.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What causes vascular lesions?

A: Vascular lesions are often caused by abnormal growth or dilation of blood vessels. They can be related to various factors, including genetics and sun exposure.

Q: Are vascular lesions dangerous?

A: In most cases, vascular lesions are harmless and don't pose a significant health risk. However, it's essential to monitor any changes and consult a healthcare professional if you have concerns.

Q: Can vascular lesions be treated?

A: Yes, certain treatments, such as laser therapy or sclerotherapy, may be used to reduce or remove vascular lesions. The choice of treatment depends on the type and size of the lesions.
