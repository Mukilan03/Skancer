Benign keratosis like lesions

Description

These growths, like seborrheic keratosis, are usually harmless. They can appear as raised, wart-like bumps or smooth, scaly patches, but they're not a cause for concern.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What causes benign keratosis-like lesions?

A: The exact cause is unclear, but they are often associated with the aging process. Genetics and sun exposure may also play a role in their development.

Q: Are benign keratosis-like lesions contagious?

A: No, these lesions are not contagious. They are non-infectious and do not spread from person to person through contact.

Q: Can benign keratosis-like lesions be removed?

A: Yes, removal is possible for cosmetic reasons or if the lesions cause discomfort. Options include cryotherapy, laser therapy, or surgical excision.
